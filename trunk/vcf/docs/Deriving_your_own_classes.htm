Deriving your own classes

Most classes in the VCF can be safely derived from. Often you'll find all you need is to derive from VCF::Object. If you think your class will need to support the firing of events and the ability of allowing other objects to respond to these events, then deriving from VCF::ObjectWithEvent may be more appropriate. In terms of size difference, Object requires 8 bytes of memory, while ObjectWithEvents weighs in at 48 bytes. There are a variety of reasons for this, almost all relating to event handling. For more information on this please see Events.
